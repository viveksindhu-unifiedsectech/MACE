## 3. How to reach them — concrete tactics

### 3.1 Warm intro path (8× higher conversion)
- Build a list of every cybersec founder + early employee who has raised
  from your target list. Use [PitchBook](https://pitchbook.com) trial,
  [Crunchbase Pro], LinkedIn Sales Navigator.
- For each, find a 2nd-degree LinkedIn connection that already follows
  you, the patent, or UnifiedSec.
- Send the intermediary a 4-sentence intro pitch + your 1-page deck
  preview + "would you be open to forwarding this to [partner]?"
- Always offer to make them a *reciprocal* intro to one of your contacts.

## 4. Funding strategy ($5M seed → Series A)

### 4.1 What $5M seed actually covers
| Bucket | $ | Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| **2 founding engineers + 1 ML/data engineer + 1 SecOps engineer** | $1.4M | Product velocity |
| **VP Sales + 1 SDR** | $700k | Pipeline + 8-12 logos in 12 months |
| **VP Engineering / CTO support** | $400k | Hire if Vivek wants to stay product-focused |
| **SOC 2 Type II audit + ISO 27001 pen-test + FedRAMP Moderate prep** | $400k | Compliance ready by M12 |
| **Cloud + tooling** | $300k | AWS + GitHub Enterprise + observability |
| **Sales/marketing (RSA + Black Hat + demos)** | $500k | Lead pipeline |
| **Legal: PCT national-phase filings (US/CA/EU/UAE/IN) + corp** | $400k | Patent moat secured |
| **Contingency** | $600k | Required by good investors |
| **General + ops + insurance** | $300k | E&O / cyber liability insurance is now table stakes |

### 4.2 Valuation guidance (May 2026 cybersec seed market)
- 1 founder + working product + patent application + 2 LOIs:
  **$15-22M pre-money** is realistic.
- Add 1 paying customer or a strong technical co-founder: **$22-30M pre**.
- With 2 named-logo paid customers ($50k+ ARR each): **$30-45M pre**.
- Strategic CVC (CrowdStrike Falcon Fund / M12) usually 25-40% premium
  to non-strategic.

### 4.3 Term sheet items to negotiate (and how)
- **Preferred equity, not SAFE** above $3M raise (cleaner Series A math).
- **1× non-participating preferred liquidation preference** is standard;
  reject any 2× or participating preferred.
- **Pro-rata rights** — agree, but cap at the lead's pro-rata.
- **Information rights** — quarterly board package + annual budget;
  reject monthly P&L below $10M ARR.
- **Reservation of board seat** — 5 people (2 common, 2 preferred,
  1 independent) is fine at seed.
- **Founder vesting reset to 4 years from close** — this is the most
  common screwjob; counter with credit for time already worked
  (typically 6-12 months credit).
- **Drag-along** — fine at >50% preferred consent.
- **Anti-dilution** — broad-based weighted average only (no full ratchet).

### 4.4 Series A trigger (M10-12)
- ARR > $1M annualised run-rate
- Net retention > 110%
- One named-logo customer at $250k+ ACV
- SOC 2 Type II report in hand
- 18-month runway minimum at A close

## 5. First-time-founder mistakes to avoid

1. **Hiring engineers before sellers.** Engineering velocity > 0 doesn't
   matter without dealflow. Sales > 0 forces engineering to be useful.
2. **Selling to your network only.** Yes you get the first 3 logos
   easily; you also learn nothing because they bought to support you.
3. **Skipping the founder mode for the chief-of-staff mode.** Until $10M
   ARR you are still the best salesperson, the best demo-er, and the
   person with the most product knowledge. Stay in every demo.
4. **Taking strategic money too early.** A CRWD or PANW CVC lead at seed
   permanently caps your enterprise prospects.
5. **Filing patents but not trademarks.** USPTO trademark on "MACE",
   "UnifiedSec", "UMEA" should cost under $1500. Do it this week.
6. **Discounting heavily for first 5 customers.** It signals weakness
   *to investors* (low ACV in the report) and to *later customers*
   ("why am I paying more?"). Give pilots, not discounts.
7. **No design-partner equity.** Offer your first 2 customers warrants
   worth 0.25-0.5% in exchange for an LOI + public logo + reference call.
   This is the cheapest customer-acquisition mechanism that exists.

## 7.5 PRE-REVENUE EXIT TRACK — $20-40M in 9-12 months

If your goal is to sell the company outright pre-revenue instead of raising
and grinding, this is the path. It is genuinely possible — but only if you
treat the next 90 days as the most important of the company.

### A. Why pre-revenue acquisitions at $20-40M happen
Acquirers buy four things at this price band, in this order:
  1. **Defensible IP** — the MACE patent (IN/2026 + PCT national-phase in
     US/CA/EU/UAE) plus the 7-domain algorithm and the UMEA agent.
  2. **A working product** — a buyer's M&A team can demo it in front of
     their CTO and verify the IP is real.
  3. **Design-partner validation** — 2 named LOIs prove someone besides
     the founder believes in the product.
  4. **The founder + 1-2 key engineers** — they want you to stay 12-24
     months on retention.

You do NOT need ARR, customers paying, or a sales team. The most common
buyers at $20-40M pre-revenue in cybersec are:

| Buyer | Why they buy pre-revenue |
|---|---|
| **Microsoft (M12 + Microsoft Security)** | Pulls patents into Defender; founder joins as PM |
| **CrowdStrike** | Falcon Fund + acqui-hires that fill product gaps (Identity, CSPM) |
| **Palo Alto Networks** | Cortex platform tuck-ins; Aoyama / Demisto pattern |
| **Cisco** | Always shopping for capabilities; Talos has acquired pre-revenue many times |
| **Trellix / Magellan** | Roll-up strategy buyer; private equity backing |
| **Sophos** | Thoma Bravo portfolio; mid-market reach |
| **Bitdefender / Acronis / Eset** | European consolidation play; mid-market |
| **F5** | Adding security capability to their app-delivery platform |
| **IBM Security** | Patent acquisition; QRadar / Cloud Pak roll-ups |
| **OpenText (CyberRes)** | Active acquirer of cyber technology |

### C. How to approach a banker — exact playbook

1. **Have these 4 documents ready** (the banker will demand them before
   taking a meeting):
   - **1-page teaser**: no logos, no founder names; just "Cyber-defence
     algorithm + cross-platform endpoint agent, US/EU/UAE patent filed,
     2 design partners, seeking strategic acquirer at $25-40M."
   - **5-slide deck**: title, problem, MACE solution, IP / patent claims,
     commercial proof (LOIs), team.
   - **15-slide full deck**: as above + competitive landscape, financial
     projections, ask.
   - **Demo video (5 min)**: scan a Mac, simulate Win/Linux/Android/iOS,
     show the dashboard, run Macey, end with patent claim language.

2. **Send the teaser to 3 bankers in parallel.** Whichever calls back
   first, you negotiate the engagement letter with — but mention the
   other two are interested. Engagement letter terms:
   - **Fee**: 3-5% of deal value, $200-400k floor.
   - **Term**: 9-12 months exclusivity.
   - **Tail**: 12-18 months after engagement ends — they still get fee
     if you sell to a buyer they introduced.
   - **Retainer**: $25-75k up-front (sometimes credited against success
     fee). Push back on cash retainer if you can; offer stock-warrant.
   - **Termination**: 30 days' notice for cause.

3. **The banker process** (months 4-9):
   - Banker calls every strategic buyer they know.
   - Buyers sign NDAs and get the data room.
   - **Letter of Intent (LOI) auction** at month 4-5 — multiple buyers
     submit term sheets; banker creates competition.
   - Pick best LOI → 60-90 days of diligence and definitive agreement.
   - Close → wire transfer + employment agreement (typically 12-24
     month retention with vesting cliff).

|---|---|---|

### E. Realistic outcome scenarios

| Scenario | Probability | Outcome to you (after taxes) |
|---|---|---|
| **No banker bites** (most likely if patent is rejected or LOIs don't materialise) | 25% | $0, but you still own the company and can raise instead |
| **Banker takes you, no buyer above $15M** | 25% | $8-12M to you after banker fee + tax (still life-changing) |
| **$25-40M close to one strategic** | 35% | $15-28M to you net |
| **Bidding war drives to $50-80M** | 15% | $30-60M to you net |

The **35% scenario is the realistic target**. Plan for it.

### F. The thing that kills these deals

The single most common reason pre-revenue exits fail: **the founder
won't commit to a 24-month retention period**. Buyers at $20-40M are
buying your knowledge in your head, not just the code. If you tell the
banker up front you want to be out in 12 months, every buyer drops out.

Decide now: 24 months at the acquirer in exchange for $20-40M is fine
(it's a really good salary for that time too — typically $300-450k +
RSU). If you can't commit to that, raise the seed instead.
